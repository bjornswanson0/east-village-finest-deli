The Corner Store

A Neighborhood Fixture

Some places just hold a block together. East Village Finest Deli is one of them — a Yemeni-American, family-run corner store that has anchored East 4th and Avenue B since 2010, where the coffee is already brewing before sunrise and the grill is still going after most kitchens have closed.

It’s the full deal: a proper deli counter, a coffee bar, fresh juices, and shelves of groceries for everything you forgot. Sandwiches built to order, egg platters that fix a rough morning, and a menu deep enough that you could eat here every day for a month without repeating yourself. Plenty of regulars do.

And the block holds it right back. This is the kind of place where the counter knows your order before you finish saying it — and when the shop hit a rough stretch in 2020, it was neighbors who came running. Through the pandemic the lights stayed on, the coffee stayed hot, and the corner never went dark.

No fuss, no lines out the door for the ’gram — just fast hands, friendly faces, and fair prices, seven days a week.
